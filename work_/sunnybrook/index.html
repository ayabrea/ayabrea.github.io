SunnyCare eMedRec Module

Electronic medication reconciliation for accreditation

My team and I designed the electronic medication reconciliation (MedRec) module to help streamline the process of obtaining a list of a patientâ€™s home medications and comparing this list to current hospital medications. The eMedRec product was officially launched in some areas of the hospital in late spring 2017 and was well-received by users, with hundreds of medication reconciliations completed as of August 2017. Users have praised eMedRec for being user-friendly, innovative and time saving.

Environmental Analysis

To map the process that presently happens on paper, I shadowed and interviewed pharmacy technicians and pharmacists at Sunnybrook. Additionally, I reviewed articles on existing implementations of electronic medication reconciliation.

Wireframes & Prototypes
Early MedRec wireframe
Updated MedRec wireframe, after usability testing.

I worked closely clinical experts to capture key functions and smooth navigation to the eMedRec module within the SunnyCare system. Using Axure RP, I created detailed prototypes of eMedRec, involving different clinical scenarios for usability testing.

Usability Testing

I conducted usability testing with pharmacists, staff physicians, residents and nurses from a variety of units including nephrology, general internal medicine and mental health. This information helped us identify areas of improvement and to redesign parts of the module before release, especially in areas where we could accommodate experienced users and along with additional support to novice users.

Development & Production

Using Mantis, I worked closely with developers to ensure that design requirements were met and that they were informed of design issues.
