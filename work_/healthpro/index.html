HealthPRO Pharmaceutical Review Process

Optimization of healthcare procurement services
User Experience, Service Design

HealthPRO is a Canadian pharmaceutical procurement company. They wanted to update their pharmaceutical reviewing software as part of their procurement process. Healthcare Human Factors discovered that we needed to examine the other aspects of the process to improve the overall experience of pharmaceutical purchasing and reviewing.

Environmental Analysis

My colleague conducted structured interviews with previous buyers to understand the pharmaceutical purchasing and reviewing process. We conducted field work at our client's offices and gathered the instructional material that they supplied to buyers.

As it turned out, the problem at hand was not simply an issue with their software. The software they used for reviewing was part of the ecology of the reviewing process. I completed a literature review to examine the current state of pharmaceutical purchasing and pharmaceutical product labelling. My colleagues researched environmental and psychological factors and carried out a heuristic analysis on the software to identify and prioritize usability issues.

Areas of Opportunity

Combined with the long reviewing process and existing usability issues with product labelling, we found that the software was not very user friendly. We determined that our challenge encompassed service redesign as well as the inclusion of human factors and user experience.

Outcomes

For our final report, we distilled themes and prioritized business decisions and rationale. I assisted in designing easy-to-read and visually appealing report for the client. I supported my colleagues in presenting results to the client in a conference call.

Our client was impressed by our multi-pronged problem solving process and we invited by client to present on human factors principles to their stakeholders at seminar.
